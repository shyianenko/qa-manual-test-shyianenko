# General QA Questions
**Question 1:** What are the key responsibilities of a QA Engineer in the software development lifecycle?
**Answer 1:** Analise product requires, test documentations, create test cases and plans, test product, create bug reports, communicate with devs and pm

**Question 2:** What do you think is more important for QA: finding bugs or preventing bugs? Why?
**Answer 2:** prevent them. they would cost less

**Question 3:** You find a critical bug on the day of a release. What steps would you take?
**Answer 3:** freeze release and report bug. communicate with pm, devs for hotfix and try to release product in time

**Question 4:** What would you do if you found a requirement that was unclear or incomplete during testing?
**Answer 4:** report to pm and make some proposals for team 

**Question 5:** How would you manage testing if the development team delivers a feature at the last minute?
**Answer 5:** test it via documentations and devs notes in "how to test"

**Question 6:** What is exploratory testing, and when is it most useful?
**Answer 6:** when we start working with new product

**Question 7:** What is regression testing, and how do you decide what areas to cover during regression testing?
**Answer 7:** test of all functional of product. we test it before every release and between releases. Prefer to cover all functional, espesialy critical cases  

**Question 8:** What is a test case, and what details should a good test case include?
**Answer 8:** TC is a case to test one functional. Good TC must have (at least) Header, Steps To Reproduce, Expected Result, envirement, priority, severity, additional screens (if needed)

**Question 9:** Have you ever worked with tools for managing test documentation? Which ones, and how did you use them?
**Answer 9:** testCaseLab, Test rail, x-ray. creating tc, tp, making scenarios for smoke and regress testing

**Question 10:** What should be included in a QA test report?
**Answer 10:** % of coverage with TC of product, how many of them passed and failed, priority, can we release or not

**Question 11:** How do you handle situations where a developer disagrees with a defect you've reported?
**Answer 11:** show them on my pc how to reproduce or record a video. Make attention on requirements and actual result

**Question 12:** How do you ensure that stakeholders understand the risk of releasing software with open defects?
**Answer 12:** it depends on priority of that defects (if it doesn't crash system - ok. like banner to small or types are Arial instead of Roman Sans ). I have to warn them, but if they release it - that's there's choise

**Question 13:** How do you decide the severity and priority of a defect?
**Answer 13:** according to the documentation and practics 

**Question 14:** You are tasked with providing a report of testing progress for a release. What would your report include?
**Answer 14:** total tc coverage, % of tested TC's, priority of TC's, passed and failed TC's in %
